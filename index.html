Historic landmarks throughout the state of Tennessee stand witness to the brave deeds of men and women who saw a chance to change their world for the better, to express themselves in a new way, or to claim a place for future generations. History in the Volunteer State is about science, architecture, natural wonders and musical greats, not to mention tall tales and ghostly legends.

Landmarks and historic places highlighted on this site include:

  • Museums, such as Chucalissa Archaeological Museum (Memphis)
  • Monuments, such as David Crockett's Statue (Lawrenceburg)
  • Historic Parks and Sites, such as Shiloh National Military Park (Shiloh)
  • Historic Buildings and Homes such as The Hermitage (Nashville)
  • Natural Wonders, such as the Lost Sea (Sweetwater)
